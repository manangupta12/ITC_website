  • Step 1: Cleaning the Dataset

    (1) Stopwords Remvoal - Removal of '.' , ',' , '!' and other stop words from the review statements of the customers.

    (2) Converting numbers to words.

    (3) Lammatization - It refers to doing things properly with the use of a vocabulary and morphological analysis of words, normally aiming to remove inflectional endings only and to return the base or dictionary form of a word, which is known as the lemma .

    (4) Word vector generation

  • Step 2: LSTM Model

    Long Short Term Memory networks – usually just called “LSTMs” – are a special kind of RNN, capable of learning long-term dependencies. LSTMs are explicitly designed to avoid the long-term dependency problem. Remembering information for long periods of time is practically their default behavior, not something they struggle to learn!

    We used an LSTM model for Sentiment Analysis and got an accuracy of around 95%.

  • Step 3: Keyword Generation

    (1)POS(Part of Speech) tagging to identify the important keywords driving the sentiments to extremes

    (2)Ordering keywords on the basis of strength.

  • Strength Calculation

    Strength of a word is defined as the distance weighted sentiment average of the nearest neighbor of the word.

    Nearest Neighbors of Nice- [Cigarette, enjoy, awesome, first, say]

    Sentiment of a word is defined as the average of sentiments of all the sentences carrying the word

    Example of strength calculation:

    Strength of Nice = Sentiment[Cigarette]*Similarity[Cigarette-Nice]+

    Sentiment[Enjoy]*Similarity[Enjoy-Nice]+ Sentiment[Awesome]*Similarity[Awesome-Nice]+

    Sentiment[say]*Similarity[say-Nice]+ Sentiment[first]*Similarity[first-Nice]

    Strength of Nice= 0.7*0.7+0.9*0.8+0.95*0.4+0.5*0.3+0.2*0.2=1.78

  • P/N Sentiment Ratio

    It is the ratio of the Positive reviews to the number of negative reviews.
